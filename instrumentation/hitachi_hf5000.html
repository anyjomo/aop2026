Instrument Overview

A Hitachi HF5000 cold-field emission gun (Cold-FEG) microscope combining STEM and TEM capabilities for extreme high-resolution imaging, elemental analysis, and nanoscale characterization of the W@WOx core-shell nanoparticles used in this study.

Manufacturer

Operating voltage
30–200 kV (default: 200 kV)

Imaging Detectors

Multiple STEM and TEM detectors for complementary contrast mechanisms:

HAADF
High-Angle Annular Dark Field — Z-contrast (I ∝ Z1.7)
ABF
Annular Bright Field — inverted contrast, light elements visible
DF (medium angle)
Dark Field — less Z-sensitive than HAADF
BF (TEM)
Bright Field — standard TEM, particles appear dark
HAADF is the primary detector for W@WOx samples: the heavy W core (Z=74) appears intensely bright, while the lighter WOx shell appears as a fainter halo, providing direct visual proof of core-shell morphology.

Analytical Capabilities

EELS
Electron Energy Loss Spectroscopy — O K-edge (~532 eV), W M4,5-edge (~1800 eV)
EDS
Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy — multiple SDD detectors
NBD
Nanobeam Diffraction (~1 nm probe) — isolates core vs. shell crystallography
SAED
Selected Area Electron Diffraction (not suitable for <15 nm particles)
For 5–15 nm core-shell nanoparticles, STEM-EELS line scans are preferred over EDS for oxygen mapping, as the sub-&angstrom; probe prevents beam broadening and can chemically profile individual particles.
